Methodology / Interpretation

How to Read FS: A Logical Process

1

Auditor's Report & KAMs

Start here. Check for Qualified Opinions, Adverse remarks, or Emphasis of Matter. Review 'Key Audit Matters' (KAMs) for risk areas.

Goal: Assess Reliability

2

High-Level P&L/BS Scan

Review Revenue & Margins (P&L) → Working Capital & Debt (BS) → Cash from Operations (CFS).

Goal: Health Check

3

Cross-Reference Notes

Don't just read numbers. Read Accounting Policies, Contingent Liabilities, Commitments, and detailed schedules.

Goal: Detail & Context

4

Internal Consistency

Perform tie-offs: BS Closing Balances vs Statement of Changes in Equity; Accruals vs Cash Flow logic.

Goal: Verify Integrity

5

Map to Law (Compliance)

Check specific sections: Sec 185/186 (Loans), Sec 135 (CSR), Sec 188 (RPTs), and Borrowing limits.

Goal: Ensure Governance

CS Perspective

A Company Secretary should not view Financial Statements in isolation. The scrutiny must connect the financial data back to the Board's Report, Minutes of Meetings, and Statutory Registers to ensure a holistic view of corporate governance.
