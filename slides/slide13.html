Monitoring Governance Signals

Related Party Transactions (RPTs): Monitor year-on-year growth in RPT volumes. A spike in RPTs (purchases/sales) relative to turnover may indicate profit shifting or governance risks requiring Audit Committee scrutiny.

Ratio Swings (>25%): Under Schedule III amendments, any change in key ratios (like Debt-Equity, Current Ratio) exceeding 25% compared to the previous year requires specific explanation in Notes to Accounts.

CSR Spend vs. Obligation: Track unspent CSR amounts over 3 years. Recurring shortfalls may trigger penalties or require transfer to unspent CSR accounts/Schedule VII funds.

Capitalized Costs Trend: Check the ratio of Capital Work-in-Progress (CWIP) to PPE additions. Long-standing CWIP without capitalization suggests project delays or potential impairment triggers.

Borrowings vs. Utilization: Compare borrowing trends with asset creation. Rising debt without corresponding asset growth may signal funds diversion or operating losses.
