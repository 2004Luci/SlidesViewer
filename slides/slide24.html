Regulatory Framework / Financial Reporting

Accounting Standards vs Ind AS

Dual Reporting Regime

Accounting Standards (AS): Applies to Non-Ind AS companies (SMCs/Non-SMCs). Follows Schedule III Division I format. Principles based on historical cost.

Indian Accounting Standards (Ind AS): Converged with IFRS. Applies to listed companies and unlisted companies with Net Worth >= ₹250 Cr. Follows Schedule III Division II (or III for NBFCs).

Key Difference: Ind AS focuses on Fair Value measurement, substance over form, and extensive disclosures compared to AS.

CS Focus Areas & Compliance

Applicability Check: Verify net worth threshold annually. Once Ind AS applies, it applies forever (even if net worth falls).

Consolidation Scope: Ind AS 110 (Control) vs AS 21. Check for de facto control over entities not legally owned but operationally controlled.

Financial Instruments: Ind AS 109 requires ECL (Expected Credit Loss) model for provisions, impacting profitability and covenant ratios.

Transition Disclosures: Ensure 'First-time Adoption' (Ind AS 101) reconciliations are approved by Audit Committee and Board.

Applicability Hierarchy

Phase I (April 1, 2016): Listed & Net Worth >= ₹500 Cr

Phase II (April 1, 2017): Listed & Net Worth >= ₹250 Cr

NBFCs and Banks (2018-2019): Specific, separate, and later dates were set for NBFCs and banks based on net worth

*Applies to Holding, Subsidiary, Associate & JV
