Governance / Audit Response

Responding to Audit Qualifications: CS Playbook

1

Identify & Assess

Distinguish between 'Qualification', 'Emphasis of Matter', or 'Adverse Opinion'. Assess legal implications under Companies Act.

Input: Audit Report Draft

2

Draft Board Response

Prepare explanations for the Board's Report (Sec 134(3)(f)). Address the root cause, not just the symptom.

Action: Clear Drafting

3

Coordinate & Align

Liaise with Management (for facts), Auditors (for understanding), and Audit Committee (for oversight).

Role: Bridge Builder

4

Disclose & Intimate

Ensure notes to accounts reflect the issue. For listed entities, intimate Stock Exchanges promptly (Reg 30 LODR).

Output: Transparency

5

Monitor Closure

Track remediation actions. Ensure the qualification does not recur in the subsequent financial year.

Goal: Clean Report

CS Role: Strategic Communication

A qualification signals a governance gap. The Company Secretary must ensure the Board's response is factual, mitigating, and demonstrates a concrete plan for rectification, thereby preserving stakeholder trust and minimizing regulatory scrutiny.
