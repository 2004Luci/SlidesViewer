Definition of Related Party Transactions (RPT)

Ind AS 24 Definition

Indian Accounting Standards (Ind AS), particularly Ind AS 24, define a Related Party Transaction (RPT) as a transfer of resources, services, or obligations between a reporting entity and a related party, regardless of whether a price is charged.

SEBI LODR Definition

Under SEBI (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, a related party transaction means any transaction involving a transfer of resources, services, or obligations between a listed entity and a related party, regardless of whether a price is charged. The term transaction includes a single transaction or a group of transactions.

Ind AS 24 – Meaning of Related Party

Under Indian Accounting Standard (Ind AS) 24, a related party is defined as a person or entity related to the reporting entity preparing financial statements. This includes individuals or close family members with control, joint control, significant influence, or key management personnel (KMP) status over the entity or its parent.

Key Persons as Related Parties: A person or close family member qualifies if they control or jointly control the entity, have significant influence, or act as Key Management Personnel (KMP). Close family includes spouse or domestic partner, children, and dependents who may influence transactions with the entity.

Key Entities as Related Parties: Entities are considered related if they belong to the same corporate group (parent, subsidiary, or fellow subsidiaries), are associates or joint ventures of the same party, or are government-related entities exercising control or significant influence.
